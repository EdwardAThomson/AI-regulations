# Singapore — AI Governance

Singapore's AI governance approach is voluntary and soft-law based, with no standalone binding AI legislation. The governance stack is layered: the Model AI Governance Framework (2020) provides the base, the Model AI Governance Framework for Generative AI (June 2024) adds a generative AI overlay, and the Model AI Governance Framework for Agentic AI (January 2026) adds an agentic AI overlay. Each extends the prior rather than replacing it. Binding obligations come through adjacent law (PDPA for personal data, HSA for medical devices, MAS supervisory expectations for financial services).

Singapore's distinctive contributions include practical testing infrastructure (AI Verify, Project Moonshot) that most jurisdictions lack, and an outsized international influence through the ASEAN Working Group on AI Governance.

## Position in comparative landscape

Singapore's approach sits between the UK's pro-innovation framework and the EU AI Act's binding regulation:

- **Voluntary frameworks layered over time** — contrasts with the EU's comprehensive AI Act but more developed than the UK's non-statutory principles
- **Testing infrastructure as differentiator** — AI Verify and Project Moonshot are practical tooling that other jurisdictions lack. The UK has established AISI as a research body but has not built comparable deployer-facing assurance tools
- **Agentic AI leadership** — Singapore's January 2026 framework is the first national-level governance document specifically for agentic AI. The UK's February 2024 consultation response discusses agentic AI but does not provide a deployment framework
- **Binding through adjacent law** — PDPA, HSA, MAS supervisory practice — similar to the UK's approach of relying on Equality Act, UK GDPR, Consumer Rights Act
- **International influence via ASEAN** — Singapore-led ASEAN Guide on AI Governance is complementary to the UK-led international scientific consensus process described in [`../UK/analysis/review-claude/10-international-scientific-report-safety-advanced-ai.md`](../UK/analysis/review-claude/10-international-scientific-report-safety-advanced-ai.md)
- **UK AISI partnership** — AISI has a formal partnership with the Singapore government (per [`../UK/analysis/review-claude/02-pro-innovation-regulation-consultation-response.md`](../UK/analysis/review-claude/02-pro-innovation-regulation-consultation-response.md))
