## Notes

- Singapore has no standalone AI-specific binding legislation. Its approach is voluntary/soft-law, relying on governance frameworks and industry collaboration.
- The governance stack is layered: general framework (2020) → generative AI overlay (2024) → agentic AI overlay (2026). Each extends the prior rather than replacing it.
- Testing infrastructure (AI Verify, Project Moonshot) is a differentiator — practical tooling most jurisdictions lack. Source documents for these tools are still needed.
- Binding obligations come via adjacent law: PDPA for data, HSA for medical devices, MAS supervisory expectations for financial services.
- Singapore's international influence is outsized via ASEAN WG on AI Governance, GPAI membership, and participation in Bletchley/Seoul/Paris summits.
- 8 of 8 collected documents have been reviewed. Remaining "Needed" items are testing tools; remaining "Lower priority" items are sector-specific and adjacent legislation.
