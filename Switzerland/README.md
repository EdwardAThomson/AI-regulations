# Switzerland — AI Governance

Switzerland has adopted a deliberately light-touch, technology-neutral approach to AI regulation. The Federal Council explicitly decided against standalone AI legislation in November 2023, preferring to address AI through existing frameworks (particularly the revised Federal Act on Data Protection) and sector-specific supervisory practice. Switzerland is not bound by the EU AI Act but faces market access pressure and has tasked the Federal Department of Justice and Police with assessing equivalence requirements.

Switzerland's distinctive contribution is its international convening role: Geneva hosts the ITU AI for Good platform, and Switzerland positions itself as a neutral hub for AI governance diplomacy.

## Position in comparative landscape

Switzerland's approach is the closest in philosophy to the UK's pro-innovation, non-statutory stance, but without the UK's dedicated safety institute or comparable scale of investment. Key features:

- **No standalone AI law** — contrasts with EU AI Act; closer to UK framework
- **Data protection as primary binding instrument** — revised Federal Act on Data Protection (revFADP / nDSG, September 2023) covers automated decision-making and profiling
- **EU equivalence pressure** — Switzerland must assess whether Swiss law provides sufficient protections to maintain market access into the EU; this is a dimension the UK also faces but handles differently given Brexit context
- **Geneva as international hub** — ITU AI for Good, UN agencies, neutral diplomatic tradition. Complements the UK's AI Safety Institute secretariat role for the International Scientific Report
- **FINMA addresses AI through existing supervisory frameworks** rather than standalone regulation — analogous to UK FCA's approach referenced in [`../UK/analysis/review-claude/02-pro-innovation-regulation-consultation-response.md`](../UK/analysis/review-claude/02-pro-innovation-regulation-consultation-response.md)
