## Notes

- Switzerland has adopted a deliberately light-touch, technology-neutral approach. The Federal Council explicitly decided against standalone AI legislation in November 2023.
- The primary binding instrument relevant to AI is the revised Federal Act on Data Protection (revFADP, Sep 2023), which includes provisions on automated decision-making and profiling.
- Switzerland is not bound by the EU AI Act, but faces market access pressure. The FDJP was tasked with assessing whether Swiss law needs amendment for equivalence — verify whether this report has been published.
- Switzerland's key differentiator is its international convening role: Geneva hosts ITU AI for Good, and Switzerland positions itself as a neutral hub for AI governance diplomacy.
- FINMA addresses AI through existing supervisory frameworks rather than standalone AI regulation.
- Several items need verification for developments after mid-2025.
