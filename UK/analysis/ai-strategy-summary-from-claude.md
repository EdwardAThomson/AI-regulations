# AI Strategy Documents: Scotland & UK — Summary

*Compiled April 2026*

---

## Scotland's AI Strategy 2026–2031

**Published:** March 2026  
**Source:** Scottish Government  
**URL:** https://www.gov.scot/publications/scotlands-ai-strategy-2026-2031/

### Overview

A five-year national strategy to harness AI for responsible and inclusive economic growth, with the goal of making a positive difference at every level of Scottish society. The strategy is explicitly values-led, rooted in Fair Work principles, and positions Scotland as a responsible innovator rather than a passive adopter.

### Delivery: AI Scotland

A new national transformation programme led by the Scottish Government in partnership with The Data Lab, ScotlandIS, and enterprise agencies. Operates across the "triple helix" of business, academia, and public sector. An Expert Advisory Board will provide oversight and strategic guidance.

### Framework: The AI Stack

The strategy is structured around the "AI Stack" — a set of non-hierarchical layers in which coordinated action must be taken. These span:

- **Trust & Ethics** — Frameworks to ensure public sector AI use is transparent and trustworthy; Expert Advisory Board established by 2027
- **Skills & Literacy** — AI literacy programmes, revitalised SME Adoption Programme, new AI Leadership Academy
- **Research & Commercialisation** — Innovation programme and national cluster scheme to bridge research and commercial impact; new university commercialisation pilot
- **Infrastructure** — Data centres designated as critical national infrastructure; UK's first AI Growth Zone in North Lanarkshire; leveraging Scotland's renewable energy capacity
- **Data** — Addressing fragmentation across systems; improved data sharing and information governance

### Key Foundations

- Five Scottish universities ranked in the UK's top 30 for AI research output (2025)
- Edinburgh hosts UKRI Doctoral training centres, the National Robotarium, and the Generative AI Laboratory
- Scotland produced 38.4 TWh of renewable electricity in 2024 — a record high — supporting energy-intensive AI infrastructure

## UK Government AI Documents

The UK government's AI strategy is distributed across several documents rather than a single equivalent strategy.

---

### 1. AI Opportunities Action Plan

**Published:** January 2025  
**Authors:** Matt Clifford (commissioned by DSIT)  
**URL:** https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/67851771f0528401055d2329/ai_opportunities_action_plan.pdf

The closest UK-level equivalent to Scotland's strategy. Sets out 50 recommendations across:

- **Compute & Infrastructure** — Building AI infrastructure, establishing AI Growth Zones for data centres
- **Data** — Unlocking public and private sector data assets
- **Skills** — Upskilling 10 million workers with AI training by 2030
- **Homegrown AI** — Investing in UK-based AI development and scale-ups
- **Regulatory environment** — Pro-innovation approach with responsible guardrails

The UK remains the leading AI market in Europe, with £6 billion in venture capital raised by domestic AI firms in 2025.

---

### 2. AI Opportunities Action Plan: One Year On

**Published:** 29 January 2026  
**Author:** DSIT  
**URL:** https://delivery.ai.gov.uk/

Progress report against the 50 original recommendations. Key headline: **38 of 50 commitments met within the first year**. Notable progress includes:

- Compute capacity grown from 2 ExaFLOPs (2024) to 21 ExaFLOPs (2025), targeting 420 ExaFLOPs by 2030
- 1 million AI training courses delivered by June 2025 (ahead of schedule)
- Focus shifting toward enabling deployment of cutting-edge AI applications and pro-innovation sandbox initiatives

---

### 3. AI for Science Strategy

**Published:** November 2025  
**Authors:** Minister Kanishka Narayan MP and Lord Patrick Vallance  

Allocates up to **£137 million** from the UK's broader £2 billion AI investment (2026–2030) to AI-driven scientific discovery. Three pillars: **data, compute, and people & culture**. Key commitment: develop trial-ready drugs within 100 days by 2030. UKRI to mandate FAIR data principles across all relevant experimental data by 2030.

---

### 4. AI Playbook for UK Government

**Published:** February 2025  
**Author:** Government Digital Service (GDS)  

Practical technical guidance for UK government departments and public sector organisations on the safe and effective use of AI. Companion to the broader strategy documents, focused on implementation rather than policy.

## Comparative Notes

| Dimension | Scotland 2026–2031 | UK Action Plan 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | Devolved; economy, public services, society | UK-wide; economic growth focus |
| Timeframe | 5-year strategy | Ongoing; annual progress reviews |
| Structure | AI Stack (layered framework) | 50 discrete recommendations |
| Delivery body | AI Scotland (new body) | DSIT; existing departments |
| Tone | Values-led, inclusive growth | Pro-innovation, growth-focused |
| Infrastructure angle | Renewables + cool climate | AI Growth Zones + compute |
| Oversight | Expert Advisory Board | AI Safety Institute |

---

*Note: The Scottish strategy is a single consolidated document; the UK approach is distributed across multiple publications with no direct single equivalent.*
