# Data (Use and Access) Act 2025: Government Guidance

**Source:** https://www.gov.uk/guidance/data-use-and-access-act-2025-data-protection-and-privacy-changes
**Published:** 2025 (guidance updated post-Royal Assent)
**Issuing body:** GOV.UK (Department for Science, Innovation and Technology)

**Note:** The Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 (DUAA) was enacted as primary legislation. This file contains the GOV.UK guidance summarising its data protection and privacy changes, plus contextual information from ICO guidance and the parliamentary record. It serves as the source document for this repository's analysis purposes; the full Act text is at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/18.

## Overview

The Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 ("DUAA") received **Royal Assent on 19 June 2025**. This comprehensive legislation includes provisions for digital verification services, Smart Data schemes (such as Open Banking), and a National Underground Asset Register. It also introduces important modifications to the UK's data protection and privacy framework.

The DUAA does not replace the UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, or the Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003. Instead, it amends these laws to:

- Simplify organisational compliance
- Foster innovation
- Support law enforcement efforts
- Enable responsible data-sharing while maintaining robust data protection standards

This guidance covers key changes but is not comprehensive or regulatory guidance. The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) publishes detailed regulatory guidance.

## Key Changes

### 1. Automated Decision-Making (ADM)

The DUAA establishes a **more permissive framework** under the UK GDPR for organisations to make decisions based solely on automated processing with legal or similarly significant effects on individuals. Organisations must implement safeguards including:

- Providing people with information about significant decisions made about them
- Enabling them to make representations about and challenge such decisions
- Enabling them to obtain human intervention in respect of such decisions

For law enforcement processing under the Data Protection Act 2018, exemptions to safeguards apply for limited reasons, such as safeguarding national security or avoiding inquiry obstruction. Exemptions require reconsideration "as soon as reasonably practicable" with meaningful human involvement.

**AI relevance:** This is the most significant AI-relevant provision. The previous Article 22 UK GDPR position (from which the AI Playbook for UK Government quotes: "decisions based solely on automated processing that have legal or similarly significant consequences" are prohibited) is significantly relaxed. Organisations now have a wider lawful base for solely automated decisions provided safeguards are met.

### 2. Subject Access

The DUAA clarifies response time limits for subject access requests. It includes a **"stop the clock" rule** allowing organisations to pause response time when needing additional requester information. Organisations must conduct reasonable and proportionate searches.

### 3. Children's Data Protection

New rules require certain online services likely to be accessed by children to consider child protection and support measures when designing these services.

### 4. Scientific Research

The DUAA clarifies that scientific research **encompasses commercial research**. It permits researchers to seek consent for broad related research areas and establishes clear safeguards for personal data use in research activities.

### 5. Recognised Legitimate Interests

The Act introduces a **new lawful ground for personal data processing**, giving organisations greater confidence to use data for crime prevention, safeguarding, emergency response, and other specified legitimate interests.

### 6. International Data Transfers

The Act simplifies and clarifies rules for transferring personal data internationally.

### 7. Responding to Complaints

The DUAA requires organisations to handle complaints from individuals concerned about data protection breaches, including providing electronic complaint forms and informing individuals about complaint outcomes.

### 8. Storage and Access Technologies (Cookies)

The Act permits storage and access technology use without explicit consent in certain low-risk situations.

### 9. Changes to Parts 3 and 4 of the Data Protection Act 2018

The DUAA amends Parts 3 and 4 regulating law enforcement and intelligence service processing. Some amendments mirror UK GDPR changes, ensuring consistency. Others simplify legislation, enabling law enforcement efficiency and closer UK intelligence agency collaboration for national security.

## Smart Data Schemes

Beyond data protection, the Act creates new powers to establish **Smart Data schemes**, allowing consumers and businesses to share data securely to access better, more tailored products and services. Schemes can help households manage finances and secure better deals, improve SME access to finance, and speed up transactions. The AI Opportunities Action Plan: One Year On notes 36 million pounds investment in supporting development of new Smart Data schemes across the economy.

## Digital Verification Services

The Act creates a framework for trusted digital verification services — relevant to AI-enabled identity systems and online services.

## National Underground Asset Register

Creates a register of underground infrastructure assets — relevant for AI Growth Zone planning and data centre development.

## Commencement

Changes to data protection law commence in stages, **2-12 months after Royal Assent** (i.e. between August 2025 and June 2026). Exact dates set in commencement regulations published on GOV.UK.
