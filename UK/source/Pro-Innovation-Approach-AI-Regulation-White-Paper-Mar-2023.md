# A Pro-Innovation Approach to AI Regulation — White Paper

**Source:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ai-regulation-a-pro-innovation-approach/white-paper
**Published:** 29 March 2023
**Issuing body:** Department for Science, Innovation and Technology (DSIT)

---

## Overview

This white paper outlines the UK's regulatory strategy for artificial intelligence. The approach prioritises enabling innovation while addressing risks through a flexible, principles-based framework rather than prescriptive legislation.

## Five Foundational Principles

1. **Safety, Security and Robustness** — Ensuring AI systems operate reliably and protect physical/mental wellbeing
2. **Appropriate Transparency and Explainability** — Making AI decision-making understandable to users and stakeholders
3. **Fairness** — Preventing discriminatory outcomes and bias in AI systems
4. **Accountability and Governance** — Establishing clear responsibility throughout the AI lifecycle
5. **Contestability and Redress** — Providing mechanisms for individuals to challenge AI decisions and seek remedies

## AI Definition

Rather than prescribing specific technologies, the framework defines AI by two functional characteristics:

- **Adaptivity**: Systems trained on data that infer patterns humans cannot easily discern
- **Autonomy**: Systems making decisions without direct human control

## Framework Characteristics

The regulatory approach is deliberately:

- **Pro-innovation** — Enabling responsible development rather than stifling it
- **Proportionate** — Avoiding excessive burdens on businesses and regulators
- **Trustworthy** — Addressing genuine risks to build public confidence
- **Adaptable** — Flexible enough to evolve with rapid technological change
- **Clear** — Providing transparency about rules, enforcement, and compliance
- **Collaborative** — Encouraging partnership among government, regulators, industry, and civil society

## Implementation Strategy

### Non-Statutory Initial Approach

The government deliberately avoids immediate legislation, instead issuing principles on a non-statutory basis. This allows:

- Existing regulators to apply principles within their sector expertise
- Flexibility to respond proportionately to different contexts
- Businesses to innovate without rigid regulatory constraints

The framework includes a monitoring period to assess effectiveness before potentially introducing statutory duties on regulators.

### Central Support Functions

Government will provide coordination through:

- Monitoring and evaluation of framework effectiveness and innovation support
- Risk assessment across the economy for emerging AI harms
- Horizon scanning to identify and respond to technological developments
- Testbed and sandbox support to help innovators navigate regulatory requirements
- Education and awareness to clarify rules and engage citizens
- International coordination to ensure compatibility across regulatory regimes

These functions operate within existing government structures rather than creating a new dedicated AI regulator.

## Key Policy Positions

### Context-Specific Regulation

Rather than blanket rules, regulation focuses on "the context in which AI is deployed." A chatbot summarising articles presents different risks than one providing medical advice. Foundation models like large language models warrant monitoring because they enable numerous applications with varying risk profiles.

### Support for Innovation

The framework commits to establishing regulatory sandboxes where innovators can work with multiple regulators to test novel applications.

## Relationship to Existing Law

The framework builds on rather than replaces existing legal structures including:

- Equality Act 2010 — Addressing discriminatory outcomes
- Data Protection — Ensuring fair processing
- Product Safety Laws — Covering physical safety
- Consumer Rights — Protecting purchasers
- Sector-Specific Regulation — Financial services, medical devices, etc.

## International Approach

The UK emphasises its ability to establish distinctive regulatory approaches while remaining committed to international interoperability. The strategy aims to position the UK as a global leader in pragmatic AI governance, responsible innovation practices, and AI assurance industries.

## Strategic Objectives

1. **Economic Growth** — Removing barriers, reducing uncertainty, and enabling investment in AI-driven innovation and productivity
2. **Public Trust** — Addressing genuine risks while protecting fundamental values like safety, privacy, and fairness
3. **Global Leadership** — Positioning the UK attractively for AI companies and talent while shaping international governance
