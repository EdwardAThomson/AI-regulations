## Notes

- The UK has no comprehensive AI Act. This is a deliberate policy choice — the approach is principles-based, sector-regulator-led.
- Five cross-cutting principles: safety, transparency, fairness, accountability, contestability.
- A statutory duty on regulators to have regard to the five principles was promised in the Feb 2024 response but has not yet been legislated.
- The Data Use and Access Bill is the most important adjacent legislation — reforms UK GDPR and includes AI-relevant provisions.
- AISI's institutional future is uncertain — transition toward possible statutory footing discussed but not confirmed.
- Compute strategy is a major differentiator: 2 → 21 ExaFLOPs in 2025, targeting 420 ExaFLOPs by 2030.
