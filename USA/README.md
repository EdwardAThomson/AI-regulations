# United States — AI Governance

The US has no comprehensive federal AI legislation. Its AI governance landscape is a patchwork: executive orders (subject to change on administration transitions), agency enforcement under existing authority, voluntary frameworks (NIST AI Risk Management Framework), and rapidly proliferating state-level legislation. The most consequential recent transition: Biden's EO 14110 (safety-first, mandatory reporting for frontier AI developers) was revoked on the first day of the Trump administration (January 2025) and replaced by a competitiveness and deregulation framing, with an AI Action Plan published July 2025.

Over 700 AI bills were introduced across state legislatures in 2024 alone, with Colorado's AI Act (SB 24-205, effective February 2026) being the landmark state-level statutory framework.

## Position in comparative landscape

The US represents the lightest-touch approach among major jurisdictions, with additional volatility from administration transitions. Key features:

- **NIST AI RMF** remains the foundational voluntary framework regardless of administration changes, and is referenced internationally (including in UK consultation response and CMA report)
- **US AI Safety Institute** (announced November 2023, housed at NIST) partnered with UK AISI — see [`../UK/analysis/review-claude/08-aisi-approach-to-evaluations.md`](../UK/analysis/review-claude/08-aisi-approach-to-evaluations.md) for UK framing of the bilateral relationship. Status of US AISI under current administration is a significant open question.
- **State-level legislation** represents a counter-current: while federal policy de-regulates, states (particularly Colorado, California, Utah) are introducing binding obligations. This creates a de facto two-track US system
- **International positioning:** US reportedly did not sign the main declaration at the Paris AI Action Summit (February 2025), a significant break from the UK-led Bletchley/Seoul process described in [`../UK/analysis/review-claude/10-international-scientific-report-safety-advanced-ai.md`](../UK/analysis/review-claude/10-international-scientific-report-safety-advanced-ai.md)

Several items in the inventory need URL and status verification for developments after mid-2025.
