# OMB Memorandum M-25-21 — Review

**Document:** M-25-21: Accelerating Federal Use of AI through Innovation, Governance, and Public Trust
**Published:** 3 April 2025
**Issuing body:** Office of Management and Budget (OMB), Director Russell Vought
**Legal status:** Binding on executive branch agencies; rescinds and replaces M-24-10
**Length:** ~30 pages (including Appendix implementation guidance)

---

## Summary

OMB M-25-21 is the Trump administration's directive for how federal agencies should adopt, govern, and manage AI. It replaces the Biden-era M-24-10 and reframes federal AI governance from risk-management-first to innovation-first while maintaining safeguards for civil rights, civil liberties, and privacy. Agencies must appoint a **Chief AI Officer**, develop public AI strategies, maintain AI use case inventories, and implement minimum risk management practices for **"high-impact AI"** — AI that could have significant impacts when deployed. If high-impact AI is not performing appropriately, agencies must discontinue its use until compliance is achieved. OMB chairs an interagency council to coordinate federal AI efforts.

---

## Key Points

### Three Priorities

1. **Innovation** — remove barriers; lean forward on AI adoption; maximise value of existing investments; invest in American AI marketplace; develop AI talent
2. **Governance** — cut bureaucratic bottlenecks; redefine AI governance as enabler; Chief AI Officer appointment; delegate risk acceptance throughout agency; interagency council chaired by OMB
3. **Public Trust** — deploy trustworthy AI; minimum risk management for high-impact AI; discontinue AI not performing appropriately; protections for civil rights, civil liberties, privacy

### High-Impact AI Requirements

Agencies must implement minimum risk management practices for AI that "could have significant impacts when deployed." When high-impact AI is not performing appropriately:
- Agencies must have a plan to discontinue use until compliance
- If proper risk mitigation is not possible, agencies must cease use entirely

This preserves the substance (though not the framing) of Biden-era risk management for federal AI use — the mechanisms survive even as the rhetorical emphasis shifts from caution to acceleration.

### Key Continuities from M-24-10

- Annual AI use case inventory
- Compliance plans
- Reporting to OMB
- Chief AI Officer designation
- Risk management for consequential AI

### Key Changes from M-24-10

- Framing: "innovation" before "governance"
- Explicitly directs agencies to remove "unnecessary and bureaucratic requirements that inhibit innovation"
- Maximise use of "AI products and services developed and produced in the United States" — a Buy American preference
- Delegates risk acceptance more broadly through agency hierarchy

---

## Observations

M-25-21 is significant because it is **binding on federal agencies** — unlike most US AI governance instruments. It creates operational requirements (Chief AI Officer, use case inventory, compliance plans, high-impact risk management) that the NIST AI RMF's voluntary approach cannot. Within the federal government, this is the most consequential operational AI governance document.

The high-impact AI requirements notably **preserve Biden-era safeguards** in substance if not in spirit. The requirement to discontinue non-compliant high-impact AI is a meaningful enforcement mechanism within government.

For firms selling AI to the US federal government, M-25-21 is the procurement-facing compliance reference. Alignment with its innovation, governance, and trust priorities is expected in federal AI contracts.
