# Executive Order: Removing Barriers to American Leadership in Artificial Intelligence

**Source:** https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/removing-barriers-to-american-leadership-in-artificial-intelligence/
**Signed:** 23 January 2025
**Issuing body:** White House (President Trump)

---

## Section 1: Purpose

The order affirms that the U.S. has maintained AI innovation leadership through "free markets, world-class research institutions, and entrepreneurial spirit." It aims to develop AI systems "free from ideological bias or engineered social agendas" and revokes existing policies viewed as obstacles to American AI advancement.

## Section 2: Policy

The foundational policy establishes that "the United States [shall] sustain and enhance America's global AI dominance in order to promote human flourishing, economic competitiveness, and national security."

## Section 3: Definition

The order adopts the statutory definition of artificial intelligence from 15 U.S.C. 9401(3).

## Section 4: AI Action Plan Development

**Timeline:** 180 days from issuance

**Responsible Parties:**
- Assistant to the President for Science and Technology (APST)
- Special Advisor for AI and Crypto
- Assistant to the President for National Security Affairs (APNSA)

**Coordination:** Economic Policy, Domestic Policy, OMB Director, and relevant agency heads must collaborate to develop an action plan implementing the stated policy.

## Section 5: Revocation Implementation

**(a) Policy Review:**
- Immediate review of all policies from revoked Executive Order 14110 (October 30, 2023)
- Identification of actions inconsistent with the new policy
- Agency heads directed to suspend, revise, or rescind conflicting actions
- Exemptions to be provided where immediate finalization isn't possible

**(b) OMB Memoranda Revision:**

**Timeline:** 60 days

**Directive:** OMB Director, coordinating with APST, must revise OMB Memoranda M-24-10 and M-24-18 for consistency with this order's policy framework.

## Section 6: General Provisions

Standard provisions clarify that the order does not impair agency authority, affect OMB functions, create enforceable rights, or supersede applicable law or budgetary constraints.
