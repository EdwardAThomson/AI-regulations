## Notes

- The US has no comprehensive federal AI legislation. The landscape is a patchwork of executive action, agency enforcement under existing authority, voluntary frameworks, and state-level legislation.
- The most significant transition: Biden's EO 14110 (safety-first, mandatory reporting) was revoked on day one of the Trump administration (Jan 2025) and replaced with a competitiveness/deregulation framing.
- NIST AI RMF remains the foundational voluntary framework regardless of administration changes.
- State-level legislation is proliferating rapidly — over 700 AI bills introduced across states in 2024 alone. Colorado's AI Act is the landmark.
- The status of US AISI under the current administration is a key uncertainty — verify whether it continues to operate.
- Several items need URL/status verification for developments after mid-2025.
